Agentic RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is an advanced variant of 
the standard RAG architecture. In typical RAG, a model retrieves relevant
 information from external data sources, like vector databases or knowledge
  bases, to augment its responses during generation. Agentic RAG extends
   this idea by integrating the model with agent-like behaviors, enabling 
   it to autonomously perform tasks, interact with APIs, and adapt its
    retrieval strategies dynamically.

Agentic RAG models act as 'agents' that not only fetch information but 
also make decisions based on the context of the query. They can navigate 
through multiple data sources, refine search queries, and even trigger API 
calls to fetch real-time information or perform transactions. This agent-like
 behavior allows for deeper contextual understanding and more robust,
  real-time interactive responses.

For example, in a conversational AI setting, an Agentic RAG could understand
 a user’s query, retrieve the necessary information from a vector database, 
 and also trigger external services (like weather APIs, news databases, or
  even financial data streams) to provide highly accurate and contextual responses.

This concept is pushing the boundaries of traditional RAG by merging it with 
aspects of autonomous agents, opening the door to more intelligent, interactive,
 and responsive AI systems.
